Climate changes

Climate change refers to the change of climate mean over time, that is, one or both of climate mean and deviation (anomaly) together appear statistically significant changes. The larger the deviation value is, the greater the magnitude of climate change is and the more unstable the climate state is. Generally refers to the change of climate state at any time scale.
They range from the longest several billion years to the shortest interannual variation. It can be divided into geological climate change, historical climate change and modern climate change. The scale of climate change in geological period is about 104 ~ 108 years, mainly based on animal and plant fossils and various traces of indirect research. It has been proved that there were several alternations between glacial and interglacial periods during the period. The scale of climate change in the historical period is about 102 to 103 years, during which human beings appeared, mainly based on historical documents, changes of flora and fauna, tree ring analysis and other means. It has experienced the alternating change process of warm period and cold period, dry period and wet period. Different regions of the world have both synchronous change and reverse change.
The scale of modern climate change is 100 to 101 years, studied using systematic meteorological records. It is generally believed that from the end of the 19th century to the first half of the 20th century, large areas of the Northern Hemisphere warmed, especially in the Arctic and high latitudes, with a significant increase in temperature, while the southern hemisphere did not change much, and the warming peaked around 1940, and began to cool thereafter. The causes of climate change are complex.
